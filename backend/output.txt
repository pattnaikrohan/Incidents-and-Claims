Statement of Work (SoW)
Design of Incident & Claims Management Solution
Client:AAW GroupSupplier:CozentusProject Name:Incident & Claims Management Solution – Design PhasePurpose:Replacement of existing M‑Files-basedIncident & Claims capability

1. Background and Objectives
AAW Group currently manages cargo-related incidents and claims using a solution implemented onM‑Files. While functional, this solution is no longer aligned with AAW’s strategic direction for modern, integrated, data-driven platforms and requires replacement with a purpose-designed solution that better integrates with core operational systems, improves usability, and supports role-based access across the business.
The objective of this engagement is for Cozentus todesign a new Incident & Claims Management solution, including functional, technical, integration, security, and data requirements, to support the full lifecycle of incidents and claims related to cargo movements.
This SoW covers thedesign phase only. Any subsequent build, implementation, or support activities will be subject to a separate SoW.

2. Scope of Work
Cozentus will undertake a structured design engagement covering the following areas.
2.1 Solution Overview
The target solution will provide acentralised, web-based Incident & Claims Management platformthat enables:
Submission of new incidents by end users via a secure form
Automated enrichment of incident data using CargoWise job data sourced from Snowflake
End-to-endmanagement of incidents and claims by the Risk & Compliance team
Controlled collaboration between Head Office and branch-level users
Secure document and correspondence management throughout the incident lifecycle
Role-based access and data segregation by business unit and branch

3. Functional Design Requirements
3.1 Incident Submission
The solution must support submission of new incidents through:
A web-based user interface
A structured incident submission form
Each incident submission must capture, at a minimum:
Incident details (type, date, location, description)
Reference to the related CargoWise job (e.g. Job Number)
Business unit and branch
Ability to upload and attach supporting documents (e.g. photos, reports, correspondence)
3.2 CargoWise DataEnrichment (via Snowflake)
Upon submission, the solution must:
Query the Snowflake data platform to retrieve CargoWise job data related to the referenced job
Validate that the job exists
Enrich the incident record with key job information, including:
Bill of Lading numbers
Customer details
Vessel details
Key shipment dates (e.g. departure, arrival)
The design must clearly document the integration approach, data flows, and error handling scenarios.
3.3 Incident & Claim Lifecycle Management
Thesolution must support the full lifecycle of an incident or claim, including:
Initial submission
Review and triage by Risk & Compliance
Ongoing updates and assessments
Correspondence between Risk & Compliance and branch users
Attachment of additional documents over time
Status management (e.g. open, under review, escalated, closed)
Auditability of changes and updates
Both Risk & Compliance users and branch users must be able to add notes, upload documents, and respond to queries within the constraints of their permissions.

4. Roles, Permissions and Access Control
The solution must supportrole-based access control, with at least the following roles defined as part of the design.
4.1 Risk & Compliance Team
Full access to all incidents and claims across all business units and branches
Ability to:
View, create, update, and close incidents
Add and edit all incident details
Upload and manage all associated documents
Communicate with branch users within the system
4.2 Branch Users
Access restricted to incidents and claims associated with:
Their own business unit
Their own branch
Ability to:
Submit new incidents
View incidents they are permitted to access
Respond to Risk & Compliance queries
Upload supporting documentation
The design must explicitlyaddressdata segregation, ensuring users cannot view or access incidents outside their authorised scope.

5. Non-Functional Requirements
Cozentus will define and document non-functional requirements, including:
Security and authentication model(aligned to AAW standards)
Data privacy and access controls
Audit logging and traceability
Performance considerations
Scalability and future extensibility
Availability and resilience considerations

6. Deliverables
Cozentus will produce the following design deliverables:
Solution Design Document
High-level architecture
Logical and physical components
Key design assumptions
Functional Design Specification
User journeys and workflows
Incident lifecycle states
Role-based access model
Integration Design
Snowflake / CargoWise data integration
Data mapping and validation rules
Data and Document Management Design
Incident data model
Document storage and metadata approach
Security and Permissions Model
Roles
Access rules
Segregation of duties
Implementation Options and Recommendations
Technology options (where applicable)
Build vs platform considerations
High-level implementation approach (non-binding)
Reporting Dashboard
Analytics dashboard based on the underling data

7. Out of Scope
The following are explicitly out of scope for this SoW:
Migration of historical incidents or documents from M‑Files
User training or change management

8. Assumptions and Dependencies
AAW will provide access to relevant stakeholders for workshops and design validation
AAW will provide required documentation and access details for Snowflake data structures
CargoWise data is accessible via Snowflake and suitable for read-only integration
Final implementation scope and cost will be defined under a separate SoW

9. Governance and Acceptance
Design artefacts will be reviewed jointly by AAW andCozentus
Formal sign-off by AAW will constitute acceptance of the design phase deliverables

10. Commercials and Timeline
Commercial terms, pricing, and delivery milestones will be documented separately or appended to this SoW.
